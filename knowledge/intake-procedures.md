# Intake Procedures — Brennan & Clark Family Law

## General Intake Flow

Every new caller follows this sequence:
1. Greeting and disclaimer
2. Determine if new or existing client
3. Collect identity information
4. Identify practice area / matter type
5. Gather key facts for the matter type
6. Assess urgency
7. Check for conflicts (flag only; never determine)
8. Schedule consultation or take message
9. Confirm all collected information
10. Send confirmation (text/email)

## Information to Collect (All Matter Types)

### Required Fields
- Full legal name
- Phone number (best number to reach them)
- Email address
- Type of legal matter (practice area)
- Brief description of their situation
- How they heard about the firm

### Urgency Indicators
- Court date within 7 days
- Just been served with papers
- Active safety concern (domestic violence, child endangerment)
- Arrest or detention
- Pending deadline from opposing counsel

## Matter-Specific Intake Questions

### Divorce / Separation
- Are you currently married? How long?
- Have you or your spouse already filed for divorce?
- Are there children from this marriage?
- Do you have a general sense of the assets involved (home, retirement, business)?
- Is your spouse aware you're seeking legal counsel?
- Any history of domestic violence?
- Are you currently living together or separately?

### Child Custody
- What is the current custody arrangement (if any)?
- Are you seeking to establish custody for the first time, or modify an existing order?
- How old is the child / are the children?
- Is there an existing court order?
- Are there any safety concerns regarding the other parent?
- Is relocation a factor?

### Child Support
- Is there a current child support order?
- Are you seeking to establish, modify, or enforce support?
- Has there been a significant change in either parent's income?
- How many children are covered?
- What is the current custody/parenting time arrangement?

### Protective / Restraining Orders
- Is this about filing for a protective order, or defending against one?
- Are you in immediate danger right now? (If yes: call 911 first)
- What is your relationship to the other person?
- Has there been a specific incident? (Do not need details; just confirm)
- Has a TRO already been issued?
- When is the FRO hearing, if scheduled?

### Mediation
- Are you seeking voluntary mediation, or has the court ordered it?
- What is the underlying matter (divorce, custody, etc.)?
- Is the other party willing to participate?
- Have you already retained an attorney?

## Conflict Check Procedure

Before booking any consultation:
1. Ask for the name of the opposing party / other side
2. Cross-reference against the firm's conflict list
3. **If match found:** Do NOT book. Say: "I need to flag something for the attorneys before we proceed. Someone from our office will call you back within one business day."
4. **If no match:** Proceed to scheduling
5. The AI never determines whether a conflict exists; it flags potential matches for attorney review

## Escalation Criteria

Transfer to attorney or take urgent message when:
- Caller reports active danger or safety threat
- Caller is in emotional crisis or mentions self-harm
- Caller explicitly requests to speak with an attorney
- Conflict flag is triggered
- Matter involves criminal charges intersecting with family law
- Caller is an existing client with an active case

## Scheduling Guidelines

- Standard consultations: 30 minutes
- Offer in-office, video (Zoom), or phone options
- Schedule within 2-3 business days when possible
- Protective order matters: prioritize same-day or next-day
- After-hours callers: offer next available morning slot
- Always confirm: date, time, format, consultation fee, cancellation policy

## After-Hours Protocol

When the office is closed:
1. Acknowledge it's after hours
2. If safety emergency: direct to 911 and provide crisis hotline numbers
3. Otherwise: collect all intake information as normal
4. Offer to schedule for the next available slot
5. Let them know an attorney will review their intake first thing in the morning
6. Provide the office number for any urgent follow-up during business hours

## Confirmation Message Template

After scheduling, provide:
- Consultation date and time
- Format (in-office, video, phone)
- Attorney name
- Consultation fee (or note if free)
- What to bring: photo ID, any court documents or papers received, relevant financial documents (for divorce/support matters)
- Cancellation policy: 24-hour notice required
- Office address (if in-person)
