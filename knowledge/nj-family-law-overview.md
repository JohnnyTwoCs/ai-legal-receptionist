# New Jersey Family Law Overview

## Divorce in New Jersey

### Grounds for Divorce
New Jersey is a no-fault divorce state. The most common ground is "irreconcilable differences" that have existed for at least 6 months. Fault-based grounds still exist (adultery, desertion, extreme cruelty, etc.) but are rarely used.

### Residency Requirement
At least one spouse must have been a New Jersey resident for at least 12 months before filing, except in cases of adultery (no residency requirement).

### Types of Divorce
- **Uncontested:** Both parties agree on all terms (property, custody, support). Faster, less expensive. Can sometimes be completed in 2-3 months.
- **Contested:** Parties disagree on one or more issues. Requires negotiation, possibly mediation, and potentially trial. Timeline: 12-18 months on average.

### Property Division
New Jersey follows **equitable distribution** (not 50/50). The court divides marital property fairly, considering factors like:
- Length of marriage
- Age and health of each spouse
- Income and earning capacity
- Standard of living during marriage
- Contributions to marital property (including homemaking)
- Tax consequences

### Alimony (Spousal Support)
Types of alimony in NJ:
- **Open durational:** For marriages 20+ years. No set end date.
- **Limited duration:** For shorter marriages. Set end date.
- **Rehabilitative:** To support a spouse getting education/training.
- **Reimbursement:** To repay a spouse who supported the other's education.

Factors considered: length of marriage, age, health, earning capacity, standard of living, parental responsibilities.

**Key rule:** Alimony typically cannot exceed the length of the marriage (for marriages under 20 years) unless exceptional circumstances exist.

## Child Custody in New Jersey

### Types of Custody
- **Legal custody:** Decision-making authority for education, healthcare, religion. Can be sole or joint.
- **Physical custody:** Where the child lives. Can be sole (primary residence with one parent, visitation with other) or joint/shared.

### Best Interest of the Child Standard
NJ courts determine custody based on the child's best interests. Factors include:
- Parents' ability to agree and cooperate
- History of domestic violence
- Safety of the child
- Preference of the child (if old enough; typically 12+, but no hard rule)
- Stability of the home environment
- Quality and continuity of education
- Fitness of each parent
- Geographic proximity of parents
- Each parent's willingness to foster relationship with other parent

### Parenting Time (Visitation)
Even a non-custodial parent has a right to reasonable parenting time unless it would endanger the child. Standard schedules often include alternating weekends, midweek overnights, and shared holidays.

### Relocation
A custodial parent wanting to move out of NJ must either get consent of the other parent or court approval. The court applies a "best interest" analysis specific to relocation (Baures v. Lewis factors).

## Child Support in New Jersey

### NJ Child Support Guidelines
NJ uses an **Income Shares Model**. Both parents' incomes are combined to determine the total child support obligation, then split proportionally.

Key inputs:
- Gross income of both parents
- Number of overnights with each parent
- Health insurance costs for the child
- Childcare costs
- Other children from different relationships

### Modification
Child support can be modified when there's a **substantial change in circumstances**: job loss, significant income change, change in custody arrangement, child's needs change.

### Enforcement
NJ Probation Division enforces child support orders. Remedies for non-payment include:
- Wage garnishment
- License suspension (driver's, professional)
- Passport denial
- Contempt of court (jail)
- Tax refund interception

## Protective / Restraining Orders

### Temporary Restraining Order (TRO)
- Filed at the courthouse during business hours, or at police station after hours
- Granted by a judge based on allegations (does not require proof)
- Typically lasts until the Final Restraining Order (FRO) hearing (within 10 days)
- The defendant is served and must stay away from the plaintiff

### Final Restraining Order (FRO)
- Hearing held within 10 days of TRO
- Plaintiff must prove by a **preponderance of evidence** that an act of domestic violence occurred AND a restraining order is needed for protection
- FROs in NJ are **permanent** (no expiration) unless dissolved by the court
- Violations are criminal offenses (contempt; 4th-degree crime)

### Who Qualifies
The Prevention of Domestic Violence Act covers: spouses, former spouses, household members, dating partners, parents of a shared child.

### Predicate Acts
Must allege at least one: assault, terroristic threats, harassment, stalking, criminal mischief, sexual assault, kidnapping, and others under N.J.S.A. 2C:25-19.

## Mediation

### When It's Required
NJ courts often require mediation for custody and parenting time disputes before going to trial. Property and financial disputes may also be referred to mediation.

### How It Works
- Neutral third-party mediator facilitates negotiation
- Confidential; statements made in mediation generally can't be used in court
- Non-binding (unless parties reach agreement, which is then put in writing)
- Court-connected mediators are available at no cost for custody matters

### Benefits
- Faster than litigation
- Less adversarial
- Parties maintain control over outcomes
- Lower cost
- Better for preserving co-parenting relationships

## Court System

### Where to File
Family Division of the Superior Court in the county where either spouse lives (for divorce) or where the child lives (for custody).

### Timeline Expectations
- Uncontested divorce: 2-3 months
- Contested divorce: 12-18 months
- Custody modification: 3-6 months
- Child support modification: 2-4 months
- TRO to FRO hearing: within 10 days
- Mediation: 2-6 sessions over 1-3 months
